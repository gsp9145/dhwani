FAQ

Questions, answered honestly.

Does my voice ever leave my Mac?

No. Transcription and AI polish run entirely on-device using Apple's speech and language models. The binary makes zero network calls — and because Dhwani is open source, you don't have to take our word for it: read the code.

Why does it require macOS 26?

Dhwani is built on SpeechAnalyzer, Apple's on-device streaming speech engine introduced in macOS 26 Tahoe — it's what makes fast, private, zero-cost transcription possible. Support for older Macs via a bundled open-source model (whisper.cpp / Parakeet) is on the roadmap.

Why does it need the Accessibility permission?

Two reasons: seeing your dictation key while any app is focused, and pasting the finished text for you. That's the same permission Wispr Flow and every dictation utility requires. Dhwani never reads your screen or logs your keystrokes — the event tap watches only for its own hotkey and Escape.

How is it free? What's the catch?

There is no catch. Cloud dictation apps charge monthly because they pay for GPU servers on every word you speak. Dhwani has no servers — Apple's models run on the Mac you already own. MIT-licensed, no account, no telemetry, no upsell.

How is this different from macOS built-in dictation?

Apple Dictation types your "um"s and false starts verbatim and offers no history. Dhwani gives you push-to-talk ergonomics, a live transcript pill, optional AI cleanup of fillers and self-corrections, hands-free mode, and a permanent local archive of everything you've dictated with daily stats.

Which languages are supported?

Around 40 locales — everything Apple's on-device speech models support, with the model for your language downloaded automatically on first launch. Additional engines for broader coverage are on the roadmap.

Can I contribute?

Please do — the project is young and moving fast. Issues, pull requests, and feature discussions are all welcome on GitHub.
